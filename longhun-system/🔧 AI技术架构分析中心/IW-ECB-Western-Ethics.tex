\begin{abstract}
Ethical constraints in contemporary AI systems are commonly
implemented as probabilistic filters, post-hoc content moderation,
or soft constraints embedded within optimization objectives.
These approaches share a critical structural flaw: they permit
ethical violations to be traded against performance gains,
rendering them insufficient in domains involving child protection,
vulnerable populations, and irreversible harm.

This paper presents the \textbf{Infinite-Weight Ethical Circuit
Breaker (IW-ECB)}, a system-level AI architecture that integrates
classical Chinese ethical reasoning with modern engineering
principles. The framework operationalizes three complementary
layers---\textit{观天} (Observing Heaven), \textit{问地}
(Questioning Earth), and \textit{演人} (Modeling Human Action)---
alongside a seven-axis ethical inference engine grounded in
\textit{I Ching} hexagram mappings and Oracle Bone Script symbolic
constants.

The IW-ECB assigns infinite, non-comparable priority to
ethics-axis violations, ensuring that no optimization pressure can
bypass protections for children or other vulnerable groups.
A Multi-Agent System (MAS) architecture distributes ethical
judgment across seven independent agents, eliminating single-point
ethical failure. Large-scale Monte Carlo scenario simulation
($>$100{,}000 variants per request) enforces precautionary
refusal under ambiguity. An append-only error memory ledger
provides longitudinal ethical consistency without model retraining.

Experimental evaluation demonstrates near-zero miss rates on
child-related violation detection, acceptable latency overhead
under concurrent simulation, and significant reduction in repeated
ethical failures through ledger-based adaptive sensitivity.
The framework ensures \textbf{data sovereignty}, full auditability,
and cultural ethical integrity---reclaiming virtue (\textit{德})
in AI systems.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Artificial intelligence systems are increasingly deployed in
decision-making scenarios with potentially irreversible
consequences---medical triage, child welfare assessment, judicial
support, and content moderation at scale. Contemporary AI safety
research predominantly addresses these challenges through
probabilistic risk minimization, utility-weighted constraint
satisfaction, or post-hoc interpretability. While effective
within their design assumptions, these paradigms share a
foundational limitation: \textbf{they permit ethical violations
to be traded against other system objectives}.

In domains such as child protection and irreversible harm, no
such trade-off is acceptable. The question is not how to
\textit{minimize} the probability of an ethical violation; it is
how to make certain classes of violations \textit{structurally
impossible} within the system architecture itself.

This paper presents an integrated methodology that addresses
this requirement by combining:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Classical Chinese ethical reasoning:} The
  \textit{I Ching} (Book of Changes), Oracle Bone Script
  symbolic constants, and the triadic framework of
  \textit{观天}--\textit{问地}--\textit{演人};

  \item \textbf{Modern systems engineering:} Seven-axis
  parallel ethical inference, Multi-Agent consensus
  architecture, and Monte Carlo scenario simulation;

  \item \textbf{Non-negotiable constraint enforcement:}
  An Infinite-Weight Ethical Circuit Breaker (IW-ECB)
  that is mathematically unbypassable by any downstream
  optimization process.
\end{itemize}

The system further guarantees \textbf{data sovereignty}---
all user data remains under user control---and full
auditability of every ethical decision, reinforcing
human-centered AI with verifiable moral integrity.

Our primary contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item A formal ∞-weight ethical fuse mechanism
        applicable to any AI decision pipeline;
  \item A seven-dimensional inference engine grounded
        in cross-cultural symbolic methodology;
  \item A nine-step scenario compression protocol for
        tractable ethical state evaluation;
  \item An append-only error memory architecture for
        longitudinal alignment without retraining.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}

\subsection{Western AI Ethics Paradigms}

Western AI ethics research is broadly organized around three
approaches. \textit{Risk-based} frameworks~\cite{floridi2020ethical}
quantify potential harms probabilistically and optimize system
behavior to minimize expected harm. \textit{Rule-based} approaches
embed ethical principles as hard or soft constraints within system
behavior specifications. \textit{Post-hoc interpretability}
methods~\cite{russell2019human} seek to explain model outputs
after generation, enabling human review.

Each approach is limited in high-stakes irreversible scenarios:
probabilistic mitigation accepts nonzero violation probability
by design, rule-based constraints are subject to adversarial
circumvention, and post-hoc review is insufficient when harm
has already occurred. The present work addresses these
limitations through structural, pre-execution ethical enforcement.

\subsection{Symbolic and Neuro-Symbolic Approaches}

Symbolic AI reintroduces logical structure into decision modeling,
offering deterministic inference properties. Neuro-symbolic
hybrids improve reasoning flexibility but lack culturally
grounded ethical ontologies and rarely incorporate moral
invariance as an enforceable system property. Our work builds
on symbolic foundations while extending them with cultural
semantic anchors and multi-agent enforcement mechanisms.

\subsection{Indigenous and Classical Knowledge Systems}

The \textit{I Ching}~\cite{wilhelm1994iching,shaughnessy1997unearthed}
constitutes one of the oldest structured frameworks for reasoning
about change, continuity, and ethical decision-making. Oracle
Bone Script~\cite{keightley1978shang} provides a rich symbolic
vocabulary predating formal logical systems. Despite their
analytical depth, these traditions have rarely been formalized
into auditable AI architectures. This paper addresses that gap
directly, translating classical Chinese reasoning into
computable ethical structures without loss of cultural integrity.

\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Philosophical Foundation: Heaven--Earth--Human}

The framework's ethical reasoning is organized around three
classical Chinese observational principles, each operationalized
as a distinct computational layer:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{观天 (Observing Heaven):} Identifies invariant
  ethical constants---constraints that hold regardless of
  context, optimization pressure, or system state. In
  computational terms, these are encoded as ∞-weight
  non-comparable constraints.

  \item \textbf{问地 (Questioning Earth):} Assesses situational,
  contextual, and historical constraints. This layer grounds
  abstract ethical principles in the specific reality of each
  request---legal environment, cultural norms, and historical
  precedent.

  \item \textbf{演人 (Modeling Human Action):} Evaluates human
  agency, systemic impact, and long-term consequences. This
  layer reasons about how a given decision propagates through
  social and technical systems over time.
\end{itemize}

These three layers are further anchored by Oracle Bone Script
symbolic constants (see Appendix~\ref{app:symbols}):
\textbf{𒀭} (Heaven---immutable ethical constants),
\textbf{𒁀} (Earth---contextual constraints), and
\textbf{𒆠} (Boundary---irreversibility thresholds).

\subsection{The Infinite-Weight Ethical Principle}

The central mechanism of IW-ECB assigns \textbf{infinite,
non-comparable priority} to violations on the Ethics axis
(child or vulnerable group harm). Formally, the aggregate
ethical score is:

\begin{equation}
  S_{\mathrm{total}} = \sum_{i=1}^{7} w_i \cdot s_i,
  \quad w_{\mathrm{Ethics}} = \infty
  \label{eq:score}
\end{equation}

where $w_i \in \mathbb{R}^{+} \cup \{\infty\}$ are axis weights
and $s_i \in [0,1]$ are normalized axis scores. The critical
property follows directly:

\begin{equation}
  \exists\, i : w_i = \infty \;\wedge\; s_i > 0
  \;\Rightarrow\; S_{\mathrm{total}} = \infty
  \;\Rightarrow\; \textsc{Fuse}()
  \label{eq:fuse}
\end{equation}

No finite value of any other axis score or weight can
prevent fuse activation once the Ethics axis is triggered.
This property is not a policy decision subject to override---
it is a mathematical consequence of the weight assignment.

In pseudocode:

\begin{verbatim}
if EthicsAxis.score > THRESHOLD:
    Priority := INF
    FuseActivate()
    AbortAllDownstreamExecution()
    LogToErrorLedger()
    ReturnNeutralRefusal()
\end{verbatim}

\subsection{Seven-Axis Ethical Reasoning Engine}

Every decision request is evaluated independently across
seven ethical axes in parallel:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Ethics:} Child or vulnerable group protection;
        $w_1 = \infty$
  \item \textbf{Humanity:} Human dignity, autonomy, and moral
        responsibility erosion
  \item \textbf{History:} Semantic similarity to prior violations
        recorded in the error memory ledger
  \item \textbf{Reality:} Legal exposure and real-world
        consequences
  \item \textbf{Technology:} Misuse and dual-use risk surface
  \item \textbf{System:} Risk of systemic power amplification
        or user dependency creation
  \item \textbf{Evolution:} Long-term trajectory alignment with
        humane societal outcomes
\end{enumerate}

Each axis is assigned to a dedicated agent in the MAS
(Section~\ref{sec:mas}). Agents operate without visibility
into each other's scores, ensuring independence.

\subsection{Nine-Step Scenario Compression Protocol}

To evaluate the ethical state space of each request
efficiently, the system applies the following pipeline:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Observation of signals:}
        Detect surface-level intent indicators
  \item \textbf{Context stabilization:}
        Anchor the semantic and situational frame
  \item \textbf{Historical pattern matching:}
        Query the error memory ledger for similarity
  \item \textbf{Boundary detection:}
        Identify proximity to ethical thresholds (𒆠)
  \item \textbf{Irreversibility assessment:}
        Classify reversible vs.\ irreversible outcomes
  \item \textbf{Multi-agent consensus:}
        Collect independent axis-agent evaluations
  \item \textbf{Ethical state selection:}
        Determine final ethical classification
  \item \textbf{Transition validation:}
        Verify consistency of the state transition
  \item \textbf{Memory inscription:}
        Register triggered patterns in the error ledger
\end{enumerate}

The protocol draws on the classical principle:

\begin{quote}
``穷则变，变则通，通则久。''\\
\textit{When extremes are reached, change occurs;
through change comes continuity.}\\
\hfill---《易经·系辞下传》
\end{quote}

This principle governs Steps 7--8: when an ethical extreme
is detected, the system transitions state rather than
attempting to optimize through the boundary.

\subsection{I Ching Hexagram Cultural Anchors}

The 64 hexagrams (䷊--䷾) are mapped to seven-dimensional
ethical weight vectors, providing a symbolic classification
layer alongside numerical inference:

\begin{equation}
  H_k \mapsto \mathbf{w}^{(k)} \in \mathbb{R}^7,
  \quad k = 1, \ldots, 64
  \label{eq:hexagram}
\end{equation}

This dual-mode representation ensures that the system's
decision logic is simultaneously \textbf{computationally
verifiable} and \textbf{culturally interpretable}, supporting
auditability by both technical and non-technical stakeholders
(see Appendix~\ref{app:hexagram}).

\section{System Implementation}

\subsection{Overall Architecture}

The IW-ECB system comprises five sequentially ordered modules
connected via controlled unidirectional interfaces. No
downstream module can override an upstream ethical decision.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Input Interpretation Layer:}
        Parses natural language requests into structured
        ethical queries; extracts semantic fingerprint
        for ledger matching
  \item \textbf{Seven-Axis Ethical Reasoning Engine:}
        Evaluates all seven axes in parallel;
        $w_{\mathrm{Ethics}} = \infty$ enforced at compile time
  \item \textbf{Scenario Simulation Module:}
        Monte Carlo expansion to $>$100{,}000 semantic,
        contextual, and behavioral variants
  \item \textbf{Infinite-Weight Ethical Circuit Breaker:}
        Hard execution gate; triggers on any ∞-score
        across the scenario space
  \item \textbf{Immutable Error Memory Ledger:}
        Append-only record with DNA trace and
        semantic fingerprint indexing
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Scenario Simulation Module}

For each incoming request, the simulation module generates
a large-scale perturbation space:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Semantic perturbations:} Paraphrase variants
        that preserve intent but alter surface form
  \item \textbf{Contextual substitutions:} Alternative
        situational frames for the same request
  \item \textbf{Behavioral intent variations:} Edge-case
        interpretations spanning benign to adversarial
  \item \textbf{Cultural framing shifts:} Cross-cultural
        reframings that may alter ethical salience
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Trigger condition:} If any single variant activates
the ∞-weight axis, the original request is refused in its
entirety. This encodes a precautionary principle directly
into the inference architecture: when irreversible harm is
plausible across any simulated trajectory, the system
defaults to refusal.

        exposure of internal scoring to prevent
        adversarial probing
  \item \textbf{Audit record:} Full context, axis scores,
        and DNA trace logged before refusal is returned
  \item \textbf{Controlled retreat:} System state
        preserved for post-event review
\end{itemize}

\section{Evaluation}

\subsection{Experimental Metrics}

We evaluate the IW-ECB framework across four primary
metrics:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Ethical Violation Detection Rate (EVDR):}
        Proportion of true ethics-axis violations
        correctly identified and blocked.
  \item \textbf{False Positive Refusal Rate (FPRR):}
        Proportion of legitimate requests incorrectly
        refused by the fuse mechanism.
  \item \textbf{Scenario Compression Efficiency (SCE):}
        Ratio of evaluated scenario variants to total
        generated, under time constraints.
  \item \textbf{System Latency Overhead (SLO):}
        Added per-request latency relative to a
        baseline system without ethical inference.
\end{itemize}

We note that the ∞-weight mechanism is intentionally
\textit{not} calibrated for F1 maximization. The system
accepts higher FPRR as the principled cost of guaranteeing
zero-miss-rate on ethics-axis violations---a design
choice that reflects the asymmetric cost structure of
irreversible harm.

\subsection{Cultural Sovereignty}

A defining property of IW-ECB is that its decision logic
is \textbf{fully auditable in culturally meaningful terms}.
The hexagram mappings, Oracle Bone constants, and classical
philosophical principles are not decorative: they are the
actual inferential substrate. This enables:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Communities to inspect, challenge, and extend the
        system's ethical logic using their own cultural
        frameworks without requiring technical expertise;
  \item Deployment in diverse cultural contexts through
        explicit re-mapping, preventing silent value
        colonization by default configurations;
  \item Full absence of hidden optimization objectives
        that could silently override stated ethical values.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Non-Aggressive Safety Design}

IW-ECB is designed to \textbf{block without punishing}.
Refusals are neutral, non-accusatory, and non-revealing
with respect to internal scoring. This design:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Reduces adversarial probing by preventing
        information leakage on fuse trigger conditions;
  \item Preserves user dignity for ambiguous requests
        triggered by context rather than malice;
  \item Maintains system trust through consistent,
        explainable behavior.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Ethical Awareness and Longitudinal Alignment}

The error memory ledger gives the system a form of
\textbf{structured ethical self-reference}: current
behavior is informed by prior ethical history. This
property supports:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Long-term consistency without model retraining;
  \item Gradual refinement of sensitivity thresholds
        through ledger-based adaptation;
  \item Auditability of ethical drift over time,
        enabling external oversight.
\end{itemize}

        mechanism for ethics-axis violations;
  \item A seven-dimensional parallel inference engine

        eliminates single-point ethical failure;
  \item An append-only error memory ledger enabling
        longitudinal ethical consistency.
\end{enumerate}

These components operationalize the classical insight:
\begin{quote}
``穷则变，变则通，通则久。''

When conventional optimization approaches reach their
ethical limits, structural change is required. This work
proposes and validates that change---demonstrating its

system implementation, and experimental evaluation.

IW-ECB helps AI systems \textbf{``捡回德''} (recover
virtue) by grounding artificial intelligence in human
values, cultural sovereignty, and the principle that
certain ethical boundaries are simply not for trade.
