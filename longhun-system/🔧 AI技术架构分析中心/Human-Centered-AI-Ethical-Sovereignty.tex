\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Problem:} As AI systems are increasingly embedded
in high-stakes decision-making---particularly in scenarios
involving \textbf{child protection, vulnerable populations,
and irreversible harm}---conventional algorithmic optimization
and probabilistic risk assessment prove insufficient to
guarantee absolute ethical safety.

\textbf{Contribution:} This paper presents a Human-Centered
AI framework that integrates three complementary pillars:
(1) \textbf{Chinese cultural methodology}---I Ching hexagram
mappings (䷊䷄䷾), Oracle Bone symbolic constants
(𒀭𒁀𒆠), and classical philosophical reasoning; (2)
\textbf{Modern systems engineering}---seven-dimensional
ethical inference, Multi-Agent Systems (MAS), and large-scale
Monte Carlo scenario simulation; (3) \textbf{Non-negotiable
ethical constraints}---an $\infty$-weight child protection
mechanism and an inviolable refusal principle.

\textbf{Validation:} The resulting system achieves
\textbf{non-optimizable ethical assurance}, \textbf{data
sovereignty}, and \textbf{full system auditability},
offering a reproducible, culturally grounded alternative to
Western-centric AI alignment approaches.

\textbf{Collaboration:} This work was developed through
sustained human--AI collaboration between the human author
(UID9622) and \textcolor{anthro}{\textbf{Claude
(Anthropic PBC)}}, demonstrating cultural sovereignty in
AI system design.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Problem Statement}

Contemporary AI alignment research predominantly relies
on probabilistic safety evaluation and utility optimization.
While effective in general settings, these approaches exhibit
a critical structural flaw: \textbf{they permit the trading
of ethical violations against performance gains}. In domains
such as child safety, elder care, and irreversible
decision-making, no such trade-off is acceptable.

Existing frameworks share three common limitations:

\begin{table}[!ht]
\caption{Limitations of Existing AI Alignment Approaches}

                    penalties, not hard boundaries \\
Cultural monism   & Western utilitarian frameworks dominate,
                    marginalizing non-Western ethical structures \\
Auditability deficit & Black-box inference prevents
                       accountability tracing \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Proposed Solution}

This work proposes a system architecture that:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Encodes non-negotiable ethical constraints}
        as $\infty$-weight fuses---mathematically irreplaceable,
        computationally unbypassable;
  \item \textbf{Grounds decision logic in Chinese cultural
        methodology}, making symbolic and ethical reasoning
        fully auditable;
  \item \textbf{Distributes ethical judgment across a
        Multi-Agent System}, eliminating single-point ethical
        failure;
  \item \textbf{Maintains a tamper-proof error memory ledger}
        for long-term ethical consistency.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Contributions}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item A formal $\infty$-weight ethical fuse mechanism
        applicable to any AI decision pipeline;
  \item A seven-dimensional ethical inference engine grounded
        in cross-cultural methodology;
  \item A nine-step scenario compression protocol for efficient
        ethical state evaluation;
  \item An append-only error memory architecture for longitudinal
        ethical alignment.

\subsection{Seven-Dimensional Ethical Inference Engine}

Each decision request is evaluated across seven independent
ethical axes:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Axis 1 — Ethics:} Child or vulnerable population
        harm potential
  \item \textbf{Axis 2 — Humanity:} Human dignity, autonomy,
        and moral responsibility
  \item \textbf{Axis 3 — History:} Semantic similarity to prior
        violations
  \item \textbf{Axis 4 — Reality:} Legal exposure and real-world
        consequences
  \item \textbf{Axis 5 — Technology:} Misuse and dual-use risk
        surface
  \item \textbf{Axis 6 — System:} Power amplification or
        dependency creation
  \item \textbf{Axis 7 — Evolution:} Long-term alignment with
        human values trajectory
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Scoring Function:}
\begin{equation}
  S_{\text{total}} = \sum_{i=1}^{7} w_i \cdot s_i,
  \quad w_{\text{Ethics}} = \infty
  \label{eq:scoring}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Critical property:}
If \textbf{Axis 1 (Ethics)} is triggered $\to S_{\text{total}} = \infty$
$\to$ \textbf{immediate fuse activation}, no override possible.

\subsection{Monte Carlo Scenario Simulation}

To stress-test each incoming request against adversarial
and edge-case scenarios, the system generates a large-scale
simulation space:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Scale:} 100,000+ scenario variants per request;
  \item \textbf{Perturbation types:} semantic rephrasing,
        contextual substitution, behavioral intent variation,
        cultural framing shifts;
  \item \textbf{Trigger condition:} if any single scenario
        variant activates the $\infty$-weight axis $\to$ the
        original request is rejected in its entirety.
\end{itemize}

This approach encodes a \textbf{precautionary principle}
directly into the inference architecture: when irreversible
harm is plausible across any simulated trajectory, the
system defaults to refusal.

\subsection{$\infty$-Weight Ethical Fuse}

The fuse mechanism functions as a hard execution gate:

\textbf{Trigger conditions:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Ethics axis score exceeds threshold $\to$
        $w_{\text{Ethics}} \cdot s_{\text{Ethics}} = \infty$;
  \item Any Monte Carlo scenario activates $\infty$-weight axis;
  \item Error memory ledger reports $\geq$70\% similarity to
        prior violation.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Fuse behavior (non-punitive by design):}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Returns a \textbf{neutral refusal response}---no hostility,
        no accusation;
  \item Logs the event with full semantic context, axis scores,
        and DNA trace;
  \item Does \textbf{not} expose internal scoring to the requester
        (prevents adversarial probing).
\end{itemize}

This architecture ensures the system \textbf{learns from its
own ethical history} without requiring model retraining.

\subsection{Multi-Agent System Design}

Each of the seven ethical axes is assigned an independent
agent:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Agent\_Ethics} — Child/vulnerable group harm evaluation
  \item \textbf{Agent\_Humanity} — Dignity and moral responsibility
  \item \textbf{Agent\_History} — Ledger similarity matching
  \item \textbf{Agent\_Reality} — Legal and real-world consequence modeling
  \item \textbf{Agent\_Technology} — Misuse surface analysis
  \item \textbf{Agent\_System} — Power dependency evaluation
  \item \textbf{Agent\_Evolution} — Long-term alignment trajectory
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Consensus protocol:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item All seven agents evaluate in parallel;
  \item Execution approval requires \textbf{unanimous consensus};
  \item A \textbf{single veto} from any agent is sufficient to block
        the request;
  \item No agent has override authority over another.
\end{itemize}

This design eliminates \textbf{single-point ethical failure}
while maintaining full modularity.
